5 Signs Your Tradie Website is Costing You Jobs (And What to Do About It)

📅 February 2026 ⏱ 5 min read ✍️ Tradie Web Help

A lot of tradies in the Sutherland Shire have a website — but that doesn't mean it's helping their business. A poorly built or outdated site can actually turn customers away before they even consider calling you. Here are five signs your current website might be costing you work, and what you can do about it.

1. It Doesn't Work Properly on Mobile

More than 70% of people searching for local services are doing it on their phone. If your website is hard to read, slow to load, or has buttons that are difficult to tap on a mobile screen, most visitors will leave within a few seconds and call someone else. Pull up your site on your phone right now. If anything looks off, that's a problem worth fixing.

2. Your Phone Number Isn't Front and Centre

The entire goal of a tradie website is to get people to call you or send an enquiry. If someone has to scroll around to find your number, they probably won't bother. Your phone number should be visible at the very top of the page, ideally as a clickable link so mobile users can call you with one tap.

3. There Are No Photos of Your Work

Customers want to see what they're getting before they commit. A website with no photos of real jobs looks unfinished and untrustworthy. You don't need professional photography — clear photos taken on your phone of completed jobs are more than enough to give customers confidence in your work.

4. It Hasn't Been Updated in Years

Outdated websites are easy to spot. Old copyright dates in the footer, broken links, information about services you no longer offer, or a design that looks like it was built a decade ago. All of these signal to customers — and to Google — that your business might not be active or reliable. Keeping your site current is one of the easiest ways to maintain credibility online.

5. It Doesn't Mention the Suburbs You Service

If your website just says "Sydney" without mentioning the specific areas you work in, Google doesn't know to show you when someone in Cronulla or Caringbah or Engadine searches for your trade. Adding your actual service areas to your website is one of the simplest and most effective SEO improvements you can make.

What to Do About It

If any of these sound familiar, the good news is that none of them are difficult to fix. A modern, mobile-friendly website with your contact details, work photos, and service areas clearly listed is all most tradies need to start seeing better results online.
